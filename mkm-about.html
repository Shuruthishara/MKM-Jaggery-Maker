We - Jaggery Maker !

We take pride in providing the noblest service for years. Established in 1988, we are expertises in our own field of sugar cane cultivation and processing.


With our 33 years of infield experiences added with the art of our ancestors, we are privileged to have learnt the complete science behind sugar processing.

We use our own farm grown sugar cane cultivated in our fields over 600 acres. The fields are spread in fertile lands of Tanjore and Vellore, drained by the boons of river Kaveri.


This helps in preventing the loss of nutrients and sugar content of the cane during travel. We choose the best sugar cane for sugar processing.

The process of sugar making is an art that involves labourers working in hazardous and primitive machineries.

It includes . . .

1. Choosing the best parts of the Cane so that the nutrients and the quality of the end product don't go compromised. The lower most part of the Cane (adikarumbu) is said to be the sweetest.

2. Extraction of cane juice. This is a very hazardous missionary and is done by practitioners. The canes are inserted in batches into a crusher machine, which will separate the cane into juice and fibre. The fibre is used for burning the cauldron later.

3. The juice is then boiled in large cauldron (kopparai in Tamil) at high temperatures to acquire the syrup which will be processed into different products.

4. It is boiled until the right consistency is attained, for which the knowledge is acquired over years of experience. This step is of much importance that, this will decide the shape and form of the end product.

5. The boiled syrup is then poured into the moulds and left to get hardened. It is then further processed into jaggery or sugar. The tilting of such a big cauldron is simplified by the innovations of our farmers. It is done with a simple pulley system.

6. All these steps requires tireless labour of many villagers. There are many a times in the past, mutilating accidents have occurred during the process.
